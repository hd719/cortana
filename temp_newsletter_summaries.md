## Morning Brew (Feb 23, 2026)

**Key Topics:**
- **Supreme Court & Tariffs:** The Supreme Court struck down Trump's tariffs, but significantly, they didn't rule on refunds - making it difficult for businesses to recover the billions they paid during the tariff period
- **Retail Security Crisis:** Online shopping has become increasingly difficult due to widespread hacker and fraudster activity targeting e-commerce platforms

## Sandy Leeds Market Update (Feb 22, 2026)

**Market Performance:**
- Stocks finished higher for the week
- S&P 500 is just 1% below its all-time high
- Fund managers now expect "no landing" scenario

**Key Economic Themes:**
1. **Fed Policy:** Rate cuts not imminent given high nominal GDP growth. Fed minutes made this clear despite productivity arguments on both sides
2. **Tariff Developments:** Supreme Court struck down Trump's tariffs but didn't rule on refunds. Administration has other options to impose tariffs. NY Fed study shows tariffs are borne by businesses and consumers
3. **Productivity Puzzle:** High GDP growth + slow employment growth = high productivity, but debate over what this means for Fed policy
4. **Global Flows:** Money flowing into Europe, dollar weakness expectations from fund managers

**Other Notable Points:**
- AI investment concerns - potential over-investment vs disruption potential
- Hyperscalers have significant unearned revenue, much from OpenAI
- Walmart seeing K-shaped economy effects
- China's export/investment reliance issues
- SF Fed: unauthorized immigrants have 1:1 effect on employment growth
- CBO projecting rising deficits and debt
- US-Iran tensions escalating, oil rallying

**Bottom Line:** Markets near highs with "no landing" expectations, complex productivity dynamics affecting Fed policy, and ongoing tariff/trade uncertainties with global capital flow shifts.
