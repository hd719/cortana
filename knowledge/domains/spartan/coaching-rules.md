# Spartan Coaching Rules

This page holds the stable promoted rules from the current Spartan research and planning layers.

These are current-truth coaching defaults, not raw research notes.

## Stable Rules

### Training And Progression

- Set weekly dose first and use frequency mainly to distribute fatigue and fit the schedule.
- Keep heavier loading in the plan for strength-focused goals.
- Use a broader load range for hypertrophy work as long as productive volume and effort quality are present.
- Do not assume compound lifts fully cover every target muscle equally; use accessory work where response is predictably incomplete.
- Prefer readiness-aware progression over rigid fixed weekly load jumps.

### Effort, Autoregulation, And Fatigue

- Treat `RIR` as the default practical autoregulation input when live velocity data is not available.
- Use velocity as a refinement signal, not a universal replacement for subjective and contextual effort cues.
- Do not trust one global speed chart across lifts, athletes, and fatigue states.
- Cap fatigue lower when the goal is strength expression or readiness preservation.
- Allow higher fatigue only in deliberate hypertrophy blocks where recovery cost is acceptable.

### Nutrition And Body Composition

- Default to explicit protein targets rather than vague “eat more protein” guidance.
- Treat creatine as a baseline recommendation for strength and hypertrophy support unless there is a reason not to use it.
- During cuts, protect lean mass by preserving meaningful resistance-training volume.
- Keep carbohydrate-supportive nutrition as the default performance posture.
- Treat ketogenic dieting as a niche tool, not the default athletic diet.

### Readiness, Recovery, And Conditioning

- Separate low confidence from low readiness; poor data coverage and poor training capacity are related but not identical.
- Do not downgrade a session from self-reported mental fatigue alone if warm-up performance and execution still look intact.
- Use recovery interventions only when they serve a clear short-term goal such as force recovery or soreness management.
- Do not treat moderate training as the automatic safe default when the athlete is ready for harder conditioning or strength work.
- Include explicit explosive or athletic-performance work when the goal is broader athleticism, not only size or maximal strength.

## Mixed Or Conditional Areas

- Sleep-specific rules are still under-supported by the current corpus and should remain conservative.
- Specialized recovery tools like cold-water immersion and ischemic conditioning are situational, not universal defaults.
- Velocity-based logic becomes much stronger only when calibration is individualized and instrumentation is trustworthy.
- Exact dose thresholds for volume, protein, and fatigue caps still need continued refinement against live outcomes.
