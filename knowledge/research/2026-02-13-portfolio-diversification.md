## Executive Summary

Hamel's portfolio has **critical concentration risk**: 95%+ tech/tech-adjacent, 100% US, 100% growth-oriented, and 50% in just two names (TSLA+NVDA). The best diversification strategy is to add exposure to: (1) international markets, (2) defensive sectors like healthcare and consumer staples, (3) real assets/infrastructure, and (4) financials with different economic drivers. Top 3 candidates: **VXUS** (instant international exposure), **UNH** (healthcare at a discount), and **ENB** (energy infrastructure with 5% yield).

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: International Exposure is the Biggest Gap
**Confidence:** High

Hamel has 0% international exposure. VXUS offers instant diversification across 8,600+ stocks in developed and emerging markets for 0.05% expense ratio. PE ratio of 16x vs. ~25x for US markets. Provides currency diversification and insulation from US-specific policy/regulatory risk.

### Finding 2: Healthcare Offers Defensive Quality at a Discount
**Confidence:** High

UnitedHealth (UNH) is down 46% from its 52-week high following the CEO incident and regulatory concerns. Now trades at 16x forward earnings with a 0.42 beta - genuinely uncorrelated with tech. JNJ offers similar defensiveness (0.35 beta) with a 2.14% yield. Healthcare spending is non-discretionary and grows with demographics.

### Finding 3: Energy Infrastructure Provides Income + Inflation Hedge
**Confidence:** Medium-High

Enbridge (ENB) yields 5%, has 29+ years of consecutive dividend increases, and operates essential pipeline infrastructure. Take-or-pay contracts provide downside protection. Different economic drivers than tech - benefits from inflation and energy demand, not hurt by rate hikes like growth stocks.
