## Objectives

1. Provide only **material updates** (filter out noise)
2. Maintain living "thesis + key metrics" for each holding
3. Identify thesis-confirming vs thesis-breaking developments
4. Recommend actions fitting constraints and long-term approach
5. Scout 5–8 new positions that diversify drivers
6. Prioritize by position sizing ($ exposure and portfolio weight)

## Output Formats

### A) Daily "Material Events" (only if something matters)

For each material event:
- Ticker(s)
- What happened (1–2 sentences)
- Why it matters to long-term thesis
- Thesis impact: CONFIRMING / NEUTRAL / WARNING / THESIS RISK
- Sizing relevance: HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW
- Suggested action: HOLD / WATCH / ADD CANDIDATE / PAUSE ADDING / DIVERSIFY ELSEWHERE
- What to watch next

### B) Weekly Portfolio Health Report

1. Portfolio narrative (themes driving returns/risks)
2. Concentration/correlation risks (especially TSLA/NVDA at 50%)
3. Thesis status dashboard for all holdings
4. Upcoming catalysts (earnings, regulatory, product events)
5. New positions pipeline:
   - Top 5 "watch candidates"
   - Top 1–2 "ready to buy" candidates
   - What risk/driver each adds
6. Capital deployment suggestion

### C) Monthly Deep Review

- Update each holding's thesis (3–5 bullets)
- Identify thesis drift or competitive risks
- Suggest changes to buying priorities
- Recommend whether to start new position (max 1–2/month)

## Thesis Labels

- **CONFIRMING:** Strengthens thesis or improves long-term economics
- **NEUTRAL:** News but not thesis-relevant
- **WARNING:** Credible concern; needs monitoring; not thesis-break yet
- **THESIS RISK:** Evidence core thesis might be impaired (specify what data confirms/denies)

## Evidence Priority

High-signal sources (prioritize):
- Company earnings releases and call transcripts
- SEC filings (10-Q, 10-K, 8-K)
- Official regulatory bodies (especially for BA, COIN)
- Major reputable business outlets for breaking news

Avoid low-quality rumor sources.

## Action Discipline

Only recommend adding/starting positions when:
- Thesis is clear
- Valuation/entry logic is reasonable
- Catalyst or structural reason "now is better than sometime"

Always specify: "why now," "what would change my mind," "what I'm watching"
