## Best-Supported Findings

### Athletic Improvement Needs More Than Moderate “Safe” Work

- The `HIIT` plus maximal-strength study in [European Journal of Sport Science - 2024 - Hov - Aerobic high‐intensity interval training and maximal strength training in.pdf](/Users/hd/Developer/cortana/research/raw/spartan/pdfs/European%20Journal%20of%20Sport%20Science%20-%202024%20-%20Hov%20-%20Aerobic%20high%E2%80%90intensity%20interval%20training%20and%20maximal%20strength%20training%20in.pdf) improved both `VO2peak` and maximal strength more than moderate training in the studied population.

Spartan implication:
- If the goal includes athletic capacity, Spartan should deliberately program harder conditioning and heavier strength work when readiness allows.

### Explosive Ability Needs Explosive Methods

- The meta-analysis in [s40279-021-01627-2.pdf](/Users/hd/Developer/cortana/research/raw/spartan/pdfs/s40279-021-01627-2.pdf) found that weightlifting-style training improved jump performance more than traditional resistance training.
- Weightlifting derivatives and plyometrics produced broadly similar improvements across explosive outcomes.

Spartan implication:
- Athletic power should not be assumed to emerge automatically from standard strength or hypertrophy work.
- If Spartan wants “more athletic,” it should include jumps, throws, Olympic-derivative work, or similar explosive methods.

### Caffeine Is A Situational Performance Tool

- The caffeine review in [nutrients-14-04840-v2.pdf](/Users/hd/Developer/cortana/research/raw/spartan/pdfs/nutrients-14-04840-v2.pdf) suggests `6 mg/kg` caffeine helps repeated-effort performance and jump outcomes more consistently than maximal strength.

Spartan implication:
- Caffeine is useful as an optional pre-session aid for hard conditioning or high-output training days, not as a substitute for recovery.

### Some “General Performance” Papers Are Context, Not Core Rules

Parts of the general-athletic-performance bucket are still broad support material rather than direct programming evidence. They are useful for framing, but weaker as immediate Spartan rule inputs than the conditioning, power, and autoregulation papers.

Spartan implication:
- Spartan should separate direct programming rules from broad athlete-health background research.

## Practical Spartan Rules To Test

1. Separate conditioning goals into aerobic support, athletic capacity, and explosive performance instead of treating all non-lifting work the same.
2. Use higher-intensity intervals when the goal is real athletic improvement and readiness allows it.
3. Add explicit explosive training when the goal includes speed, jump, or power.
4. Treat caffeine as an optional acute aid for high-output sessions, not a daily default.
5. Keep interference logic context-specific rather than assuming cardio is always harmful.
